CLOUD-MAP

Collaboration Leading Operational UAS Development for for Meteorology and Atmospheric Physics

CLOUDMAP is a research project using the combined effort of four colleges. The University of Oklahoma is working with students and professors from the School of Meteorology and with Aviation by flying UAVs to measure the boundary layer of the atmosphere in order to receive more detailed information of the boundary layer.

This effort focuses on the development and implementation of unmanned aircraft systems and their integration with sensors for atmospheric measurements on Earth with an emphasis on Meteorology and Atmospheric Physics (MAP). This includes platform and autonomous systems development, sensors and remote imaging for weather sensing, and operations in the NAS. While broad in scope, the topics are linked by common platforms and onboard systems to provide useful data related to a number of NSF goals. Enabling meteorological measurements to increase our understanding of Earth’s complex atmospheric dynamics serves as the key focus in this collaboration.
